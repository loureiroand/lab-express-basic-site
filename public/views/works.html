Works

Death Proof

Death Proof is a 2007 American exploitation horror film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. The film centers on a psychopathic stunt man, who uses his "death proof" cars to kill young women. The film is Tarantino's homage to the exploitation films of the 1970s, specifically slasher films and car chase films.

Django Unchained

Django Unchained is a 2012 American revisionist Western film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. The film stars Jamie Foxx as Django, a freed slave who becomes a bounty hunter and sets out to rescue his wife from a brutal Mississippi plantation owner. The film is a tribute to Spaghetti Westerns and Blaxploitation films.

The Hateful Eight

The Hateful Eight is a 2015 American Western film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. The film takes place in post-Civil War Wyoming and follows a group of strangers who seek refuge from a blizzard in a stagecoach stopover. The film is a tribute to classic Westerns and Agatha Christie's mystery novels.

Inglourious Basterds

Inglourious Basterds is a 2009 war film written and directed by Quentin Tarantino. The film tells an alternate history story of two plots to assassinate Nazi Germany's leadership, one planned by a young French Jewish cinema proprietor, and the other by a team of Jewish-American soldiers led by Lieutenant Aldo Raine. The film is a tribute to Spaghetti Westerns and war films.
